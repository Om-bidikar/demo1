The Morning

Before the world knew it was your day

It always does — finds you before anyone else does. That quiet window of a few seconds where it's just you and the ceiling, the room still soft with sleep, the world holding its breath before it remembers what day it is.

But today is different, Chikuu. Today the universe quietly made a note — somewhere in that space between stars and mornings — and circled it in invisible ink. Because today is the day you arrived, and nothing has been quite the same since.

Some days arrive softly. They slip in like early light through a curtain, and you only notice once you're already inside the warmth of them.

That's today. That's your birthday.

Chapter Two

On everything you carried this year

Every year asks something of you. This one was no exception. Mornings that felt heavier than they should, decisions made in the half-dark, moments where the right answer wasn't written anywhere you could find it.

And yet here you are — carrying it all without it breaking you. Turning the sharp things into something quieter, something learned, something that now fits where before it felt impossible to hold.

That's not a small thing, Chikuu. That's the whole thing.

03
The Small Things

The moments no one will write about but you

Not the milestones. Not the photographs taken for keeping. The other things — the cup of tea that tasted exactly right, the song that arrived at precisely the moment you needed it, the laugh that surprised even you with how whole it felt.

These are the chapters no biographer ever collects. But they are, in truth, the entire story. The texture of a beautiful life is made of exactly these — ordinary moments, held with the kind of quiet care only you know how to give.

You don't need to do extraordinary things to live an extraordinary life. You only need to be fully, gently present for the small ones.

Chapter Four

What the next chapter holds for you

Nobody knows. That's precisely the point. If we knew how every story ended, we'd skip to the last page and miss the part where it gets interesting.

So here's what I wish for you, Chikuu — in the year ahead: more of those unexpected turns. More doors that open sideways. More of the version of you that walks through them without waiting for permission.

You've always been that person. This year, let the whole world catch up.
